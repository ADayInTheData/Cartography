About

I’m Adam Day, an Environmental Professional with a background in conservation science and a passion for the natural world. After earning my B.S. in Environmental Studies from Roanoke College in 2019, I spent the last five years applying my skills across both the private and public sectors.

In 2025, I completed a fully online, one-year GIS Certification Program (16 credit hours) through Cal Poly, Humboldt | California, where I explored a wide range of geospatial concepts, developed technical proficiency with GIS tools including ArcGIS Pro and ENVI, and learned to design clear and effective cartographic maps. This experience deepened my interest in conservation-based spatial analysis and encouraged me to continue growing professionally.

This portfolio showcases my work across three key areas: Geospatial Analysis, Cartographic Design, and Remote Sensing. My projects explore topics like habitat fragmentation, water quality, land use change, and more.
